Tribute to Eric Nam

Eric Nam is a South Korean-American singer, songwriter, and media personality known for his influence in both K-pop and global entertainment. Born on November 17, 1988, in Atlanta, Georgia, he initially pursued a business career, graduating from Boston College in International Studies.

Music Career

Starting modestly in K-pop, Eric gained recognition for his smooth vocals and heartfelt songs. Notable works include:
  • "Ooh Ooh" (2014) featuring Hoya from INFINITE
  • "Honestly" (2018), blending K-pop and Western pop influences
  • "Before We Begin" (2019), his first all-English album aimed at a global audience

Media and Entertainment Career

Beyond music, Eric is a popular media personality, co-hosting "After School Club" and interviewing stars like Jamie Foxx, Emma Stone, and Benedict Cumberbatch, bridging Korean and Western media.

Podcasts and Entrepreneurship

Eric hosts the podcast "K-pop Daebak Show" and another called "I Think You're Dope." In 2021, he co-founded DIVE Studios, focusing on cultural diversity and global storytelling.

Achievements

He was listed in Forbes' "30 Under 30 Asia" (2017) and has completed several world tours, performing in North America, Europe, and Asia.

Activism and Personal Life

Eric advocates for mental health awareness and Asian representation, using his platform to address issues like racism and mental health struggles. He continues to be a prominent artist, entrepreneur, and advocate globally.

Achievements & contributions

  1. Global Music Success: Eric Nam has successfully bridged the gap between K-pop and Western pop, releasing his first all-English album, "Before We Begin" (2019), and embarking on multiple world tours, gaining international recognition as a global artist.
  2. Media and Cultural Bridging: Through his work as a television host and interviewer, he has helped connect Korean entertainment with Western media, interviewing major stars like Jamie Foxx, Emma Stone, and Benedict Cumberbatch.
  3. Entrepreneurship and Cultural Advocacy: Eric co-founded DIVE Studios in 2021, a media company promoting cultural diversity and global storytelling through digital content and podcasts, furthering his impact as both an artist and entrepreneur.
